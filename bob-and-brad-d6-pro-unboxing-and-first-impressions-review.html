Neuromuscular Junction — The neuromuscular junction is the specialized synapse where a motor neuron communicates with a skeletal muscle fiber, converting electrical nerve impulses into mechanical muscle contraction. When an action potential arrives at the motor nerve terminal, it triggers the release of the neurotransmitter acetylcholine, which crosses the synaptic cleft and binds to receptors on the muscle fiber membrane, initiating contraction. Dysfunctional neuromuscular transmission can contribute to muscle spasms, trigger points, and excessive muscle tension. Percussion therapy may influence neuromuscular function by stimulating sensory receptors that modulate motor neuron activity, potentially explaining the muscle relaxation effects observed after massage gun treatment.

Muscle Spasm — A muscle spasm is a sudden, involuntary contraction of one or more muscles that can cause significant pain and temporary loss of function. Spasms can result from muscle fatigue, dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, nerve irritation, or direct trauma to the muscle tissue. The mechanism involves an abnormal excitation of the motor nerve or the muscle fiber itself, leading to sustained contraction without the normal relaxation phase. Percussion therapy can help resolve muscle spasms by increasing blood flow to the affected area, stimulating the Golgi tendon organ to trigger reflexive relaxation, and providing sensory input that interrupts the spasm cycle through the gate control mechanism.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should you use a massage gun before or after foam rolling?
Many fitness professionals recommend using a massage gun before foam rolling, as the percussion helps break up superficial adhesions and increase blood flow, making subsequent foam rolling more effective. Alternatively, using the massage gun after foam rolling can target specific trigger points that the roller could not address. Both sequences offer benefits, so personal preference and specific treatment goals should guide your approach.
How do you use a massage gun on the shoulder?
To treat the shoulder with a massage gun, focus on the deltoid, trapezius, infraspinatus, and supraspinatus muscles using a round or flat attachment. Avoid direct contact with the acromion process and other bony landmarks of the shoulder joint. Work in slow, sweeping motions across the muscle belly at a moderate speed setting for 60 to 90 seconds per muscle.
Can percussion therapy help with tennis elbow?
Percussion therapy can be used as part of a treatment approach for tennis elbow by targeting the forearm extensor muscles rather than the elbow joint itself. Using a massage gun on the forearm muscles and upper arm can help reduce tension that contributes to lateral epicondylitis. The treatment should be combined with eccentric strengthening exercises and activity modification for best results.
