Optimal Settings for Different Tissue Depths

Speed settings on a massage gun should cover a range wide enough to address different therapeutic needs. Low speeds between 1,200 and 1,800 RPM work for warm-ups, sensitive areas, and light recovery. Medium speeds around 2,000 to 2,400 RPM handle everyday muscle maintenance and moderate soreness. High speeds above 2,800 RPM target deep tissue work, stubborn knots, and intense post-competition recovery.

Stretching and Flexibility — Stretching is a physical exercise in which a specific skeletal muscle or muscle group is deliberately elongated to its fullest length in order to improve the muscle's elasticity, achieve comfortable muscle tone, and increase flexibility. The major types of stretching include static stretching, where a position is held for a period of time; dynamic stretching, which involves controlled movement through range of motion; and proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation (PNF), which combines contraction and relaxation with passive stretching. Research has demonstrated that applying percussion therapy before static stretching can enhance flexibility gains by reducing muscle resistance to elongation. The combination of percussion therapy and stretching has become a popular warm-up protocol among athletes and fitness professionals seeking to maximize range of motion safely.

Scar Tissue Formation — Scar tissue forms as part of the body's natural healing response to injury, surgery, or inflammation, consisting primarily of type I collagen fibers laid down in a disorganized pattern compared to the parallel arrangement of normal tissue. While scar tissue serves the essential function of closing wounds and providing structural continuity, its irregular fiber arrangement can lead to reduced flexibility, restricted range of motion, and altered biomechanics in the affected area. As scar tissue matures over weeks and months, controlled mechanical loading and soft tissue mobilization can help remodel the collagen fibers into a more functional alignment. Percussion therapy devices can contribute to this remodeling process by delivering focused mechanical stimulation that helps break down excessive adhesions and promote more organized tissue repair.
