Electromyography — Electromyography (EMG) is a diagnostic technique that measures the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles using electrodes placed on the skin surface or inserted into the muscle tissue. EMG is used in clinical and research settings to evaluate muscle function, diagnose neuromuscular disorders, and assess the effects of various therapeutic interventions on muscle activity. Studies using EMG have demonstrated that percussion therapy can reduce muscle activation levels in hypertonic (overly tense) muscles, providing objective evidence for the muscle-relaxing effects reported by massage gun users. Research has also shown that brief percussion therapy application before exercise can increase voluntary muscle activation, supporting its use as a pre-workout preparation tool.

Deep Tissue Massage — Deep tissue massage is a therapeutic technique that applies sustained firm pressure to reach the deeper layers of muscle and fascia, targeting knots, chronic tension patterns, and adhesions that form in muscles due to injury, overuse, or stress. The technique involves slow, deliberate strokes that work across the muscle grain, progressively breaking down adhesions and restoring normal tissue texture and movement. Unlike Swedish massage, which primarily aims for relaxation, deep tissue massage addresses structural and functional problems in the musculoskeletal system. Percussion therapy devices replicate some aspects of deep tissue massage by delivering focused mechanical force that can penetrate to deeper tissue layers, though they lack the adaptive palpation skills of a trained therapist.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is stall force and why does it matter in a massage gun?
Stall force refers to the amount of pressure you can apply to a massage gun before the motor stalls or stops. Higher stall force means the device can maintain its percussive action even when pressed firmly against dense muscle tissue. This is particularly important for deep tissue work on large muscle groups like the glutes, quadriceps, and hamstrings.
What is the amplitude of the Bob and Brad D6 Pro?
Amplitude refers to how far the massage gun head travels back and forth during each percussion stroke. The Bob and Brad D6 Pro offers a generous amplitude that allows for deep tissue penetration. Higher amplitude generally translates to a more effective deep tissue massage experience.
What is the difference between a massage gun and a TENS unit?
A massage gun provides mechanical percussion to physically manipulate muscle tissue, while a TENS unit delivers low-voltage electrical currents through electrode pads to interrupt pain signals. Massage guns are better for muscle knots, tightness, and recovery, while TENS units are primarily used for pain management. Some people benefit from using both modalities as part of a comprehensive self-care approach.
Is percussion therapy safe during pregnancy?
Pregnant women should consult their obstetrician or midwife before using any percussion therapy device. While some practitioners allow gentle use on the upper back and shoulders during the second and third trimesters, the abdomen, lower back, and legs should be avoided due to the risk of stimulating contractions or affecting blood flow. Every pregnancy is different, so professional medical guidance is essential.
